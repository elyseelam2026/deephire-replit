Christian Brun is the Co-Chief Executive Officer and Founding Partner of Wellesley Partners, a global executive search and advisory firm that he has helped build since its inception in 2004. Based in Singapore, and frequently working between London and Hong Kong, Christian has been instrumental in expanding Wellesley’s presence across Asia-Pacific and Europe. He has led numerous senior-level executive search assignments in both the financial services and technology sectors, while heading the firm’s Corporate & Investment Banking Practice. In 2005, he relocated from London to Hong Kong to establish Wellesley’s Asia business, a move that marked a significant milestone in the firm’s international growth.

In addition to his leadership at Wellesley, Christian serves as Senior Advisor to Serendipity Capital, a specialist investment firm focused on disruptive themes including artificial intelligence, sustainability, quantum technologies, and financial services innovation. Since 2019, he has worked closely with the team to support long-term investments, providing strategic insight, capital partnerships, and hands-on guidance to portfolio companies with world-changing potential.

Christian’s earlier career was rooted in executive search in London, where he worked at several leading firms. He began at Whitehead Mann in 1997, before joining The Consulting Group, which was later acquired by TMP, where he served as Associate Director. In 2000, he became a Principal at Heidrick & Struggles, focusing on investment banking and capital markets across Europe, a role that deepened his expertise in global financial talent markets and laid the foundation for his entrepreneurial path.

He was educated at the University of Cambridge, where he earned both a Master of Arts in History and an M.Phil. in International Relations, receiving a College History Scholarship. Prior to that, he attended Eton College, where he achieved top academic results with three A-levels and a Distinction at S-level.
